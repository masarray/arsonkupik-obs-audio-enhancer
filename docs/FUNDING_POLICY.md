# Funding policy

ArSonKuPik OBS Audio Enhancer is free and open source under GPL-3.0. Financial support helps sustain development, testing, release engineering, documentation, and long-term maintenance, but it does not change the project's license or create a paid edition.

## Principles

- Donations and sponsorships are voluntary.
- The complete public plugin remains available without payment.
- Payment does not unlock private binaries, exclusive DSP features, or closed presets.
- Sponsors do not purchase technical decisions, security exceptions, or control of the roadmap.
- Financial support does not guarantee a specific feature, deadline, compatibility result, or priority bug fix.
- Maintainers retain final responsibility for architecture, audio quality, safety, licensing, and release decisions.
- Anonymous support is welcome.
- Public recognition is opt-in only.

## Official support channels

The official support-development page is:

https://masarray.github.io/arsonkupik-obs-audio-enhancer/support.html

The repository Sponsor button should point to that page so supporters have one verified entry point for global and Indonesian payment options.

Planned official channels:

- **Global:** GitHub Sponsors for one-time or recurring support.
- **Indonesia:** a verified merchant QRIS code.
- **Optional local platform:** one verified Saweria or Trakteer profile may be added later if it improves accessibility.

Do not send money to account numbers, QR images, phone numbers, or links shared outside the official repository and product site unless the same destination is explicitly confirmed on the official support page.

## Use of funds

Support may be used for:

- Windows and Linux build validation;
- OBS compatibility testing;
- packaging and installer maintenance;
- CI and release automation;
- audio-quality regression testing;
- measurement and monitoring tools;
- development hardware or software directly related to the project;
- domain, hosting, or project infrastructure costs;
- documentation, translation, and accessibility improvements;
- focused development time for maintenance and new public features.

Funding is not restricted to a specific expense unless a campaign explicitly says so. The maintainer may allocate support where it produces the greatest project benefit.

## Supporter recognition

Supporters may request recognition in [`SUPPORTERS.md`](../SUPPORTERS.md).

Recognition rules:

- listing is optional;
- anonymous support is fully respected;
- names, organizations, and links are published only with permission;
- unsafe, misleading, illegal, discriminatory, or unrelated links may be declined;
- recognition does not imply endorsement by the project;
- the project may remove a listing when requested or when it creates a legal, security, or reputational risk.

Suggested recognition groups are Supporters, Sustainers, and Project Backers. These labels recognize contribution level but do not grant governance rights.

## Corporate sponsorship

Organizations may sponsor development, testing, documentation, or release infrastructure. Any public corporate acknowledgment must be agreed separately and must not imply that the sponsor owns, certifies, or officially endorses the project.

A sponsor may propose work through the normal issue process, but acceptance and design remain subject to the same technical, licensing, security, CPU, and audio-quality standards as every other contribution.

## Refunds and disputes

Donations are normally treated as voluntary support rather than payment for a specific deliverable. Refund availability depends on the payment platform, applicable law, and the circumstances of the transaction.

Do not use donations to purchase consulting, custom development, guaranteed support, or commercial licensing. Those arrangements require a separate written agreement outside the donation system.

## Taxes and records

Supporters and maintainers are responsible for their own tax, accounting, reporting, and regulatory obligations. Platform receipts and transaction records should be retained as required by the relevant jurisdiction.

This policy is project governance guidance and not legal, tax, or financial advice.

## Privacy and security

- Never publish bank account details, identity documents, tax identifiers, payment API keys, or private transaction records in the repository.
- QRIS images must come from a verified merchant account and be checked for the correct displayed recipient before publication.
- Payment pages must use HTTPS and an official provider domain.
- The project does not ask for stream keys, GitHub tokens, passwords, or remote access as part of a donation.
- Supporters should redact transaction identifiers before posting public screenshots.

Report suspected payment-link tampering privately under [`SECURITY.md`](../SECURITY.md).

## Policy changes

This policy may evolve as the project grows or payment platforms change. Material changes should be committed publicly with a descriptive message. The project should prefer clarity and a small number of verified support channels over a long list of unverified payment options.
